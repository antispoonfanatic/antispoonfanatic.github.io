About Maya Angelou

Born

April 4, 1928
St. Louis, Missouri, USA

Died

May 28, 2014 (aged 86)
Winston-Salem, North Carolina, USA

Famous Works

I Know why the Caged Bird Sings, And Still I Rise, “On the Pulse of Morning”

Maya Angelou, in her early years has worked as a singer, dancer, actor, composer, and Hollywood’s first female black director, and later became most famous as a writer, editor, essayist, playwright, and poet. She also worked as a teacher as the Reynolds professor of American Studies at Wake Forest University. As a civil rights activist, Maya Angelou worked for Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and Malcolm X.
