About Susil Mandal

John Keats (1795–1821) is considered to be one of England’s greatest poets along with the likes of Wordsworth and Spenser. And this in just the 25 years that he live. (Imagine if he’d lived till the ripe old age of 90.) He wrote all his major poems in the last four to five years of his life.

When we read about Keats as a Romantic poet, (which he did not like to be called as one) we cannot help but be hit by the realisation that his own life had all the hallmarks of a tragic Romantic hero—a tragic love story, a poet who wasn’t appreciated when he was alive, a fear of his works being forgotten, a short life, death away from home. Before dying, Keats expected his poetry to be forgotten, as the epitaph he wrote for his tombstone indicates: “Here lies one whose name was writ in water.”
